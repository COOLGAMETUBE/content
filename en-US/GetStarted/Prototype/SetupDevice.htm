Setup your device

For any device you choose to set up, check to make sure your development machine is running Windows build 10.0.14393 or newer and has developer mode enabled.

SD Card based device eMMC Flash based device Dragonboard NOOBS

Flashing IoT Core

The Windows 10 IoT Core Dashboard is the best way to set up and connect to your Windows 10 IoT Core devices, all from your PC. You will use the Dashboard to flash IoT Core onto your device, and to access it once it is connected to your network and much more.

By downloading and using the Windows 10 IoT Core Dashboard, you agree to the license terms and privacy statement for Windows 10 IoT Core Dashboard.

Download Dashboard
  • Insert a compatible micro-SD card into your device, then open the Dashboard and click on Set up a new device tab.
  • Go through and populate the fields for Device Type and OS Build.

    • NOTE: To use the Insider Preview version of IoT Core, select Windows Insider Preview from the OS Build dropdown box and sign in with your Insider Program credentials. Sign up for Windows Insider Preview here.

  • Ensure that your SD card is selected in the Drive dropdown box.
  • Enter a unique name for your device as well as an administrator password. 

    • NOTE: The default name and password is minwinpc and p@ssw0rd. We recommend you change it. 

  • If available, choose a wireless network to which your device will connect.
  • Click Download and Install after accepting the software license terms.
  • Once the Dashboard has flashed the SD card, plug the card into your device, optionally plug in an HDMI display, and power on the device. After a few minutes you should see the IoT Core default application appear on the display.

Connecting to network

NOTE: Skip this step if you've already connected your device to a network in Step 1.

Wired

If the device comes with [an Ethernet port | USB Ethernet adapter support] to enable a wired connection, attach an Ethernet cable to connect it to your network.

Wireless

If the device supports Wi-Fi connectivity and you've connected a display to it, follow these instructions:

  • In the IoT Core default application, click the Settings button next to the clock.
  • On the Settings page, select Network & Wi-Fi via the menu on the left.
  • Your device will begin scanning for wireless networks.
  • Once your network appears in the list, select it and click Connect.

If you haven’t connected a display and would like to connect via Wi-Fi, follow these instructions instead:

  • In the Dashboard, click on the My Devices tab
  • Find your unconfigured board from the list. Its name will begin with "AJ_" (e.g. AJ_58EA6C68).  If you don't see your board appear after a few minutes, try rebooting your board.
  • Click on Configure Device and enter your network credentials. This will connect your board to the network.

Connecting to your developer machine

Windows Device Portal

Use Windows Device Portal to connect to your device through a web browser. The device portal provides configuration and device management capabilities, as well as advanced diagnostic tools. 

Others

You also can use PowerShell to connect to your device through a command shell. You can also follow the SSH instructions to use SSH to connect to your device.

It is highly recommended that you update the default password for the Administrator account. Instructions are included in the relevant documentation for Windows Device Portal or Powershell.

UNDER CONSTRUCTION

Flashing IoT Core

To install Windows 10 IoT Core onto your boards (Raspberry Pi) using NOOBS, you'll need to have a micro SD card formatted with NOOBS. If you already have a SD card pre-imaged with NOOBS, you can skip to learning more about connecting to a network.

Otherwise, you'll need to download the latest version of NOOBS from the official NOOBS downloadsite.

Once NOOBS has been downloaded, you must copy it onto your SD card. To do so, you'll need to mount your SD card onto your machine and format it. The SD Formatter 4.0 tool is the suggested tool to use when formatting your SD card for NOOBS.

Then, extract the NOOBS archive onto your newly formatted SD card. 

Connecting to network

Insert your NOOBS-ready micro SD card into your device. Ensure a monitor, keyboard, and mouse are connected. Connect your device to the Internet by Ethernet cable and boot your device. Otherwise, follow the on-screen instructions to connect to your wi-fi network.

Select Windows 10 IoT Core from the list of available options on the NOOBS screen, and then click install. NOOBS will ask you to confirm if you wish to overwrite the contents of the SD card before installing Windows 10 IoT Core. Unless you have data on the card you wish to save, press yes. This action cannot be undone.

While we recommend selecting the IoT Core Insider build to receive the latest Windows builds, you can also select the official-release build of Windows. Once you've selected your build, you should get the following screen.

After a few minutes, the installation should be complete and you will have rebooted into Windows 10 IoT Core.
